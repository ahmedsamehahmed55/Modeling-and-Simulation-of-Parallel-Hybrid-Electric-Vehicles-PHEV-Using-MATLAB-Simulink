# Modeling and Simulation of a Parallel Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV) Using MATLAB/Simulink

## Overview

This project focuses on the **modeling, simulation, and performance evaluation of a Parallel Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV)** using **MATLAB/Simulink**. The objective is to accurately replicate real-world drivetrain behavior and evaluate the benefits of hybridization in terms of **fuel efficiency** and **energy management**.

The simulation framework integrates **mechanical drivetrain modeling**, **powertrain control logic**, and **energy flow management**, providing a comprehensive platform for analyzing hybrid vehicle operation under different driving conditions.

---

## Project Highlights

### Powertrain Modeling

Developed detailed simulation models for **conventional internal combustion**, **fully electric**, and **parallel hybrid powertrains** in MATLAB/Simulink. The drivetrain includes a **six-speed gearbox** and a **torque-coupling mechanism** to realistically represent power flow and vehicle dynamics.

### Control Strategy & Stateflow Implementation

Designed and implemented **Stateflow-based supervisory control algorithms** to manage:

* Regenerative braking
* Engineâ€“motor mode switching
* Power split between the internal combustion engine and electric motor

These control strategies ensure **smooth power transitions** and **optimal energy recovery** during braking events.

### Performance & Efficiency Evaluation

Simulation results demonstrate an approximate **30% reduction in fuel consumption** for the parallel hybrid configuration compared to a conventional internal combustion vehicle, validating the effectiveness of the proposed hybrid architecture and control strategy.
